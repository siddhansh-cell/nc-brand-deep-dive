Key takeaways

1
NC sales down -8.3% (£531k → £487k) — broad-based decline, not a single-brand pullback. 272 brands declined vs 206 that grew. Mid-July demand softness compounded by OOS on key lines. Total lost revenue hit £120k across 214 brands. The decline is wider than any single driver — suggests underlying demand softness heading into mid-summer.
2
OOS remains the biggest drag — £120k lost revenue, with Oura Ring (£16.8k), Momentous (£5.7k), and Balance Phone (£5.3k) the largest misses. All three are preorder or supply-constrained. Oura Ring has August retro being set up — stock availability will determine whether it converts. Balance Phone preorder dates pushed to late August. Momentous has 10 SKUs OOS with iHerb compounding the pressure.
3
Margin improving despite sales decline — NC margin up +1.08pp to 33.91%. Driven by order mix (+0.82pp) as high-margin brands (Oura Ring, Balance Phone, HigherDOSE) contribute more to the mix even at lower volumes. Pricing contribution +0.21pp. Retro spend slightly down (£7.4k → £6.9k). Two consecutive weeks of margin improvement (+0.76pp then +1.08pp).
4
SURI is the standout growth story — 4× in 4 weeks (£3.2k → £13.5k) without retro support. Pricing advantage flowing through cleanly. BON CHARGE also on a 4-week growth streak and now #3 in redlight brand priority — but needs more push to Prospects and email campaigns plus the missing Red Light Cap SKU.
5
Pricing pressure continues across the portfolio — 5 of the top 10 decliners are pricing-driven. COROS MAP restrictions, nama D2C undercutting (ongoing since March), Blueprint subs discount negotiations (4th consecutive decline), MaryRuth’s pricing drag, and Metagenics losing share in cheapest. Comp enforcement remains unresolved for several brands despite repeated escalations.

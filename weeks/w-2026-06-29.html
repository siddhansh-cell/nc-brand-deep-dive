Thorne +£4,802 (+15%)

#1 NC brand, new high at £36.0k. No retro, no pricing changes. Best NC week in the dataset. LSKU implementation being tested on Thorne first (label/SKU system overhaul). Consistent top performer delivering without promotional support - the definition of structural demand. Three-week trend: £33.8k → £31.2k → £36.0k.

Hyperice +£4,111 (+76%) Retro

Retro: 23 Jun-5 Jul, 10% exclusive. Three-week uptrend: £6.5k → £5.4k → £9.5k. Retro is the primary driver - 10% Prime + exclusive pricing through the first week of July. Weekend discounting also performing well (noted w/c 18 May). Best week in the dataset. Watch for pullback after 5 Jul.

BON CHARGE +£3,726 (+146%) Retro T3 redlight

Retro: 01-31 Jul, 25% full month. #3 priority redlight brand. The +146% is retro-driven - July window started 1 Jul, likely with pre-positioning/anticipation in the w/c 29 data. Recovery from £2.6k to £6.3k. Full-month retro gives sustained runway - watch for continued growth through July. Missing SKU (Red Light Cap) still not on Healf despite being flagged Mar 2026.

Nordic Naturals +£3,591 (+44%)

Strong momentum since inclusion in 4for3. Best week in the dataset at £11.7k. Three-week uptrend: £8.2k → £8.1k → £11.7k. No pricing issues flagged. 40 new SKU labels ready for printing (28 Jun), suggesting assortment expansion in the pipeline.

Clinique La Prairie +£3,153 (+1,169%) Retro

Cymbiotika +£2,677 (+231%) RRP hold

LMNT -£6,126 (-25%)

COROS -£3,029 (-13%) MAP enforced

No retro. Strict MAP brand. Giving back some of the w/c 22 peak (£22.7k) - but £19.6k is still well above the w/c 15 level (£16.7k). Comps (CJ Performance, Balfes Bikes, Team Cycles) still discounting as of 17 Jun despite repeated escalations. PO inbound flagged for priority check-in at warehouse. Organic demand remains strong despite pricing constraints. Less concerning than it looked on uncleaned data.

Oura Ring -£1,526 (-7%) Post-retro RRP hold Lost revenue

One of the largest lost-revenue brands on the OOS treemap. Three-week decline: £28.3k → £20.6k → £19.1k. Stock availability is likely suppressing sales alongside the RRP hold. Had Prime Day retro (23-26 Jun) but it didn't generate a visible bounce (unlike WHOOP +68%). Holding RRP while Amazon, Boots, Currys discount up to 30% on Ring 4. Ring 5 quality issues surfacing (battery life, discolouration). Brand "avoiding the topic" on comp enforcement. The decline is a mix of post-retro, stock gaps, and pricing disadvantage.

Withings -£2,915 (-39%) Retro Ops delay

Retro: 27 Jun-8 Jul exclusive - but ops delay flagged. Karina flagged 1 Jul that the exclusive promo was not live, ops actioning ASAP. Third consecutive decline: £11.9k → £7.4k → £4.5k. The -39% may be partly explained by the retro not actually going live on time. If ops resolves quickly, a bounce in w/c 6 Jul is possible. Worth chasing to confirm the promo is now active.

Aarke -£2,642 (-27%) Retro MAP enforced

Declining despite active retro (23 Jun-19 Jul). Four consecutive declines: £14.5k → £11.9k → £9.9k → £7.2k. Brand enforcing strict MAP with structured matching rules. High volume of customer feedback on kettle quality issues (stops working, defective units). Product quality concerns may be suppressing demand - the retro isn't enough to offset.

HigherDOSE -£2,139 (-37%) Post-retro

Key takeaways

1
Nearly flat (-0.8%) despite WHOOP giving back £12.1k post-Prime Day. WHOOP's -£12.1k post-retro normalisation was offset by broad-based supplement gains: Thorne (+£4.8k), Nordic Naturals (+£3.6k), Momentous (+£2.2k), Metagenics (+£2.1k). The supplement category is now the stabiliser while tech wearables are volatile around retro windows.
2
Thorne hits a new NC high of £36.0k with zero retro support. The #1 NC brand delivered its best week in the dataset purely on structural demand. No pricing changes, no retro. LSKU implementation being tested on Thorne first. This is the benchmark for what organic demand looks like when the brand is healthy.
3
July retro pipeline is stronger than June. BON CHARGE (25% full month), Momentous Chews (40% - up from 25%), Therabody (rolling monthly through EOL), and The Ultimate Human (BOGO on Test Kit) all start in July. But retro gaps remain: LMNT, Nordic Naturals, and Thorne are all top 10 NC brands with no retro. Combined NC potential exceeds £65k/week.
4
Aarke and Withings declining despite active retros - different root causes. Aarke: four consecutive weekly declines (£14.5k → £7.2k) despite 23 Jun-19 Jul retro - kettle quality complaints may be suppressing demand. Withings: exclusive retro (27 Jun-8 Jul) wasn't live on time - ops delay flagged 1 Jul. One is a demand problem; the other is an execution problem. Chase Withings ops status immediately.
5
Oura Ring's trajectory is the key strategic question. Three-week decline (£28.3k → £20.6k → £19.1k) while holding RRP against 30% comp discounts. The Prime Day retro didn't generate a bounce (unlike WHOOP). Brand relationship "sensitive" but the commercial cost of holding RRP is mounting. COROS (-13%) is holding up much better under similar MAP constraints - the difference is Oura's comp pricing gap is 3× wider.

LMNT -£8,755 (-35%) Gap

No retro secured for June. Gave back the w/c 22 peak (£25.4k) - now at £16.6k, below the w/c 15 level (£18.0k). COROS -£6,614 (-29%) MAP enforced Gap

Key takeaways

1
Post-Prime Day and post-retro correction drives the -9.5%. WHOOP (-£13.3k), LMNT (-£8.8k), and COROS (-£6.6k) account for £28.6k of the £52.3k total decline. All three are normalising after w/c 22 peaks - WHOOP from Prime Day retro, LMNT from seasonal hydration peak, COROS from organic demand spike. The decline is largely mechanical, not structural.
2
POAS declining for 4 straight weeks (1.17 → 0.89) - efficiency is deteriorating. PMax LTV spend up +43% WoW but POAS collapsed to 0.81. NC revenue fell £24k short of target while PM spend exceeded by £17k. The PMax LTV scaling thesis is not working at current levels. Brand-level view: the top 3 decliners are all PMax-heavy tech brands, suggesting the over-scaling is concentrating on brands with natural demand ceilings.
3
Supplements holding steady while tech corrects. Thorne (+4%), Pure Encapsulations (+1%), Momentous (+11%), Nordic Naturals (+38%), Metagenics (+46%), NOW Foods (+22%) all stable or growing. The decline is concentrated in tech wearables and high-ticket categories. Supplements are the bedrock - consider shifting more retro budget here.
4
Oura Ring structural decline needs attention. Third consecutive weekly decline (£28.3k → £20.6k → £16.8k). Holding RRP while Amazon, Boots, Currys discount up to 30% on Ring 4. Ring 5 quality complaints surfacing. Brand "avoiding the topic" on comp enforcement. Relationship labelled "sensitive". At current trajectory, Oura drops out of the top 5 NC brands within 2-3 weeks.
5
Retro gaps and pricing constraints are the biggest headwinds. LMNT, COROS, Blueprint, and Nordic Naturals are all top NC brands with no retro support and pricing constraints (MAP, no-discount, comp matching limitations). Combined NC potential exceeds £45k/week. Securing July retro windows for even 2-3 of these brands would materially offset the post-Prime Day correction.
6
Aarke decline despite active retro is a red flag. Three consecutive weekly declines (£14.5k → £11.9k → £9.9k → £6.5k) despite having a Prime Day retro running (23 Jun-19 Jul). High volume of customer feedback on kettle quality issues. MAP enforcement limits pricing flexibility. Worth investigating whether product quality is suppressing demand.

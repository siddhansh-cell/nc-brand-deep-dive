Key takeaways

1
Overall NC down 12.9% (£476.5k to £415.1k) - significant WoW decline. Driven primarily by Oura Ring (-£10.4k), Thorne (-£6.6k), and Healf own brand (-£3.2k) which together account for £20.2k of the £61.4k total decline.
2
Oura Ring 5 launch imminent - revealed 28 May, pre-orders same day, release 4 June. The -53% WoW decline (£19.8k to £9.4k) is likely demand deferral rather than structural weakness. Comp discounting on Ring 4 may also reflect retailers clearing stock ahead of the new model. Expect NC to recover post-launch.
3
Funded promos not yet lifting NC as expected. SURI (-23%), BON CHARGE (-5%), Perifit (+45% but tiny base of £400). Promos may take >1 week to show NC impact, or are primarily driving Existing Customer sales. Worth monitoring in w/c 25 May.
4
Sneaky weekend discounts performing well. Hyperice (+47%, +£3.5k) and Aarke (+15%, +£1.3k) both cited as strong performers from weekend discounting.
5
LMNT structural recovery (+36%, +£4.5k) with no pricing changes. Demand bounce-back after 2 weaker weeks. Positive signal for a top NC brand.
6
Driver split this week. Gainers led by structural demand (LMNT, LYMA, WHOOP recovery) and pricing actions (Hyperice, Aarke weekend discounts, Zeus price reductions). Decliners driven by structural demand shifts (Oura Ring 5 deferral, Thorne, Transparent Labs) and pricing pressure (Pure Encapsulations, Momentous iHerb comp, Humantra Boots undercutting).

Oura Ring +£13,934 (+74%) Retro Lost revenue

July retro secured (01-31 Jul, same terms as Prime Day). Massive bounce from £18.9k to £32.8k — best week since the Ring 5 launch period. However, pricing team confirmed they still can’t match on key Ring 4 colourways (Black, Rose Gold, Silver, Stealth) even with the retro SOA. Amazon and Boots continue to discount 20-30% below Healf. Lost revenue: £37.4k across 8 OOS SKUs — more than the £32.8k actually sold. The retro is converting but availability is the binding constraint. #1 on the lost revenue table.

SURI +£5,807 (+135%)

More than doubled to £10.1k — best week in the dataset. Three-week uptrend: £3.2k → £4.3k → £10.1k. The June 15% retro ended 26 Jun, so this growth is happening without retro support. Noted as a pricing drag (-0.03pp) in the latest margin update, suggesting competitive pricing pressure. One SKU (Sunset Bloom) flagged as discontinued but still visible on site. Strong organic demand signal worth investigating.

LMNT +£5,030 (+27%)

Demand-led rebound. Back to £23.4k after the CRM-push-inflated w/c 22 (£24.5k) and the w/c 29 dip (£18.4k). No retro. Settling at a natural demand level around £20-24k/week. Raspberry salt batch quality complaints still being flagged. Germany orders need duplicate checking.

WHOOP +£4,041 (+19%) Post-retro

Post-retro recovery. Normalising after the Prime Day spike and subsequent correction: £20.0k → £33.6k → £21.5k → £25.6k. Now settling above the pre-retro baseline. Comp (a1techdeals) undercutting still under investigation.

Hyperice +£3,670 (+39%) Retro

Retro: 23 Jun-5 Jul, 10% exclusive. Four-week uptrend: £6.5k → £5.4k → £9.5k → £13.2k. Best week in the dataset. Retro ended 5 Jul — w/c 6 Jul captures the final days. Watch for post-retro pullback next week.

Therabody +£1,805 (+35%) Retro

Rolling monthly retro started 1 Jul (repeats through Dec, “until EOL”). Bounce from £5.1k to £6.9k as the July window opened. First brand doing a rolling multi-month retro commitment. Comps still discounting but enforcement deliberately soft-pedalled — brand is on the verge of going with Healf for the EU launch. TheraFace PRO margin concern persists.

Balance Phone +£1,801 (+39%) OOS £7.3k

Three-week uptrend: £2.5k → £4.6k → £6.4k. Strong newer brand continuing to perform. Best week in the dataset. Lost revenue: £7.3k from just 1 OOS SKU — more than the £6.4k sold. Availability is the ceiling on this brand.

Pvolve +£2,923 (+353%)

Bounced from £0.8k to £3.8k. Had a June retro (15-27 Jun, $4K spend cap). Stock coming in — POs clearing duty this week. High-ticket fitness equipment, inherently lumpy.

Key takeaways

1
Oura Ring July retro drives the biggest single-brand move (+£13.9k), but £37.4k in lost revenue from 8 OOS SKUs dwarfs actual sales. Retro secured for the full month, but pricing team confirmed Healf still can’t match Amazon/Boots on Ring 4 Black, Rose Gold, Silver, and Stealth due to margin. The retro is converting — £32.8k is the best week since Ring 5 launch — but Oura is #1 on the lost revenue table with more lost (£37.4k) than sold (£32.8k). Both pricing and availability are capping the upside.
2
Post-retro declines account for 7 of the top 15 decliners — the retro cycle is compressing. Our Place (-62%), Clinique La Prairie (-100%), Nurosym (-57%), Metagenics (-44%), HigherDOSE (-74%), Kiki Health (-19%), and Momentous (-14%) are all unwinding ended retros. The aggregate post-retro drag offset the gainers, keeping the headline flat despite strong tech wearable recovery.
3
SURI triples to £10.1k without retro support — best week in the dataset. The June 15% retro ended 26 Jun, so this is organic demand. However, SURI is flagged as a pricing drag in margin updates and one SKU is showing as discontinued while still visible on site. Worth investigating the demand driver and the margin impact.
4
Tech wearables rotate back in: Oura (+74%), WHOOP (+19%), Hyperice (+39%). After two weeks of post-Prime Day correction, tech brands are recovering — led by Oura’s July retro and Hyperice’s extended window. COROS (+9%) is steady despite MAP constraints. The category mix is shifting back toward tech after supplement dominance last week.
5
OOS is the biggest drag on the top 20: 6 brands in the table have lost revenue exceeding 50% of actual sales. Oura Ring (£37.4k lost vs £32.8k sold), Balance Phone (£7.3k vs £6.4k), HigherDOSE (£7.2k vs £965), Momentous (£12.1k vs £12.2k), Transparent Labs (£10.5k vs £9.2k), and Pure Encapsulations (£11.7k vs £16.0k). Thorne has £19.0k lost across 38 SKUs. Margin improved +0.76pp WoW to 36.30% as retro windows close, but stock availability is the binding constraint on growth.

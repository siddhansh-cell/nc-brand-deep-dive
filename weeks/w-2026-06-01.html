Key takeaways

1
NC up 13.9% (£483.3k to £550.4k) - strong recovery. Note: w/c 25 May included the bank holiday sale (25-26 May) which may have pulled demand forward for some brands, making this week's recovery even more notable.
2
Oura Ring 5 driving demand (+18%, +£4.0k). Ring 5 revealed 28 May, now live on Amazon at £399. NC recovering as new model generates excitement. Matching reverted to protect relationship - worth monitoring whether Healf stocks Ring 5 and at what terms.
3
BON CHARGE post-promo cliff (-76%, -£6.6k). 25% promo ended 27 May. Dropped to £2.1k which is below pre-promo baseline. Expected normalisation but depth of drop warrants monitoring. Missing SKU (Red Light Cap) still not listed.
4
Therabody recovering despite no-discount stance (+39%, +£2.3k). Comps still discounting, brand still not enforcing (flagged again 3 June). Underlying structural demand is strong. Validates holding RRP.
5
Broad-based recovery - 12 curated gainers vs 7 decliners. Top NC brands (LMNT +9%, WHOOP +10%, Pure Encaps +38%, COROS +35%) all growing. Decliners concentrated in promo endings (BON CHARGE) and persistent pricing pressure (Nama, Blueprint, Maurten).
6
Comp enforcement remains the biggest systemic issue. Therabody, Maurten, COROS, Nama, and Bare Biology all have comps flagged repeatedly (some for months) with no brand enforcement. This is a portfolio-level drag on NC sales.

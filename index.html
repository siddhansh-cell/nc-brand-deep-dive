Prime Day blackout context

Amazon Prime Day brands cannot be discounted before 23 Jun - discounting earlier disqualifies them from Prime Day. This is why WHOOP, Oura Ring, Our Place, Aarke, and Hyperice retros all start on 23 Jun. Pre-23 Jun softening on these brands (Aarke -17%, Oura -27%) is expected - customers are holding purchases for the Prime Day window.

July retro gap

Only 7 brands confirmed for July retros (BON CHARGE, Hyperice, Therabody, Withings, Aarke, Blueair, Hunter & Gather). 93 of the top 100 have no July retro confirmed. Steph, Conor, and Justin's sections have almost nothing confirmed for July. Priority chases: Fellow (T1, -53% this week), COROS (T1, strong demand but no retro), Blueprint (T1, growing organically), Form Nutrition (T1, gap), Designs for Health (T1, gap).

Key takeaways

1
Flat week overall (-0.8%), but the composition shifted dramatically. Tech brands surged on Prime Day (WHOOP +£13.6k, COROS +£5.9k, Our Place +£3.4k, Garmin +£3.1k) while supplements and health brands pulled back as retro windows closed (Pure Encaps -£4.1k, Biocare -£2.2k, Bare Biology -£1.3k). Net effect: tech gains offset supplement losses almost exactly.
2
Healf Zone is the biggest single mover (-£9.9k, -41%). This is Healf's own product and the decline isn't explained by external factors. Needs investigation - was there a campaign end, ad spend pullback, or landing page change w/c 22?
3
Oura Ring continues to slide (-27%, third consecutive decline). Ring 5 transition creating demand deferral. Healf holding RRP per brand partner instruction while comps discount Ring 4 up to 30%. The Prime Day retro (23-26 Jun) should provide a bounce in next week's data, but the structural Ring 4 → Ring 5 overhang remains.
4
Fellow collapsed (-53%) and is now the most urgent T1 retro gap. Went from a steady £7.6k to £3.6k with no clear external driver. Combined with the existing T1 gap status (Karina's section), this brand needs immediate attention. No retro, no pricing change, just demand falling away.
5
Nurosym is the retro success story of the week. The 10% co-funded retro (Conor) has driven a three-week uptrend from £3.2k to £7.9k (+148% over the retro window). One of the best retro-to-sales conversion rates this cycle. Window closes 24 Jun - expect pullback, but the signal is clear: co-funded retros on niche tech brands convert well.
6
COROS and Blueprint are growing strongly with no retro support. Both T1 gaps. COROS +35% to £22.7k despite strict MAP enforcement. Blueprint +31% to £8.6k (third consecutive week of growth). These brands have genuine organic momentum - securing retros for July would amplify what's already working.
7
Several brands are declining within active retro windows. Withings (-38% despite retro), Biocare (-35% despite 20% retro), Metagenics (-32% despite stacked retros), Therabody (-11% despite retro + comp matching). This pattern suggests either retro fatigue, comp undercutting eroding the retro's effectiveness, or the initial retro spike pulling demand forward.
8
Prime Day brands should bounce next week. WHOOP (+68%), Our Place (+114%), and Garmin (+247%) already showing uplift from the 23-26 Jun window. Aarke (-17%) and Oura Ring (-27%) were in pre-retro softening but their retros started 23 Jun. Expect net positive swing from tech brands in the w/c 29 Jun data.
9
SURI 25% Prime discount is a live opportunity. Karina secured the discount but it needs to go via codes/checkout, not Google Shopping. Brand Payments sheet confirmation still pending (Prarthna flagged). Once resolved, this is a strong CRM push candidate - email and SMS. Don't delay.
10
July retro coverage is critically low. Only 7 brands confirmed vs 93 gaps in the top 100. Justin's section has 0 confirmed July retros. Priority chases: Fellow (T1, just collapsed), COROS (T1, strong organic demand), Blueprint (T1, growing), Form Nutrition (T1, gap), Designs for Health (T1, gap), LMNT (no retro, £25k brand). The gap between June's 40/100 and July's 7/100 will hit NC hard if not closed.
